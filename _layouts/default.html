About Me

Hello. I recently completed my PhD in Experimental Nuclear Physics from Florida State University in Tallahassee, Florida, United States. My PhD work focuses on studying nuclear structure using state of the art gamma-ray spectrometers combined with scintillator charged particle detector array. I developed end to end data analysis pipeline from the Pixie16 Digital Data Acquisition system. I analyzed terabytes of data generated from the stable beam on the fixed target experiments at the John D. Fox Laboratory, a nuclear physics experiment laboratory at FSU.

Prior to my PhD, following the completion of my undergraduate program, I have a chance to join data integration and automation team to assist a company to perform digital transformation into a Data Lake. This experience introduced me about the private sector as well as how to coordinate with multiple stakeholders within the team, across the teams as well as users. My undergraduate work focused on studying the stability of stars within modified gravities using configuration entropic method which is inspired by Shannon-Information theory.
